Introduction

This mini-course "Effective English Grammar" helps students build a clear understanding of basic English grammar and use it confidently in real-life communication. The focus of this lesson is the verb "to be" (am / is / are) and personal pronouns.

The material is organized like a small online learning module: each section includes theory, practice, self-study tasks, and a short test so students can check their progress.

  • 🎯 Apply grammar knowledge in short texts about people, feelings, jobs and places.

📚 Topic 1 · Theoretical Section

Verb "to be" · am / is / are

Verb "to be": am / is / are · Personal Pronouns with "to be"

The verb "to be" is one of the most important verbs in English. We use it to talk about identity, description, jobs, nationality, place and feelings. In the present simple tense, "to be" has three forms: am, is and are.

A. Talking about identity & description

👤 People, things, jobs and nationalities

We use the verb "to be" to say who or what someone or something is, and what they are like.

  • I am a teacher. (job)
  • She is tall. (physical description)
  • They are students. (who they are)
B. Talking about feelings or conditions

😊 Emotions, states and temporary conditions

We also use "to be" to describe how someone feels or their current state.

  • I am happy. (feeling)
  • He is tired. (condition)
  • We are ready. (state)
Personal Pronouns with "to be"

🧩 Pronoun + correct form of "to be"

Each personal pronoun has a specific form of the verb "to be" in the present simple.

Personal pronoun Form of "to be" Example
I am I am happy.
You are You are my friend.
He is He is a doctor.
She is She is from Spain.
It is It is a nice day.
We are We are in the park.
They are They are at home.

Remember: I → am, you / we / they → are, he / she / it → is.

🧠 SRO · Self-study Tasks

Independent work

These tasks are designed for independent work. Students can complete them at home or in a self-access learning session.

  1. 1 Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) about yourself using the verb "to be". Include information about your name, age, nationality, job or studies, and where you are now.
  2. 2 Create 10 sentences using personal pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, they). Use different pronouns and different forms of "to be".
  3. 3 Complete additional exercises online (teacher provides links or platform). Focus on choosing the correct form of am / is / are.
  4. 4 Take a short self-assessment quiz about personal pronouns and the verb "to be". Write down which questions were difficult and review the theory again.

Tip for students: After finishing, read your sentences aloud to practice pronunciation and fluency.

📑 Methodological Instructions

For the teacher

This section provides a step-by-step plan for teaching the lesson on the verb "to be" with personal pronouns.

  • 1. Introduction of grammar rules. Start with simple examples on the board. Underline the pronouns and the verb "to be". Elicit the forms am, is, are from students and create a summary table together.
  • 2. Presentation of examples. Use sentences about real students (names, jobs, hobbies, nationality) to make the examples meaningful and personal. Read the sentences aloud and ask students to repeat.
  • 3. Fill-in-the-blank exercises. Move to controlled practice (Exercise 1). First, complete one or two items together as a class, then let students work individually. Check answers in pairs and then as a whole group.
  • 4. Matching and transformation activities. Use transformation tasks (Exercise 2) or matching (pronoun ↔ form of "to be") to deepen understanding. Encourage students to say the sentences aloud after they write them.
  • 5. Short worksheet with mixed tasks. Combine different types of tasks (gap-fill, sentence ordering, correction of mistakes) in a short worksheet. Use it as a mini-test or as preparation for homework and SRO.

Suggested mixed worksheet (example)

  • Part A: Fill in 6 sentences with am / is / are.
  • Part B: Correct 4 incorrect sentences (e.g., She are a doctor → She is a doctor).
  • Part C: Match 7 pronouns to the correct form of "to be".
  • Part D: Write 5 new sentences about classmates using the correct forms.

Finish the lesson with a short reflection: ask students which forms are easy or difficult and which situations they can now talk about using the verb "to be".
